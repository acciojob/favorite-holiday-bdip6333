Kolkata


Kolkata, the capital of West Bengal, is a vibrant city known as the "City of Joy" for its rich cultural heritage, intellectual legacy, and warm spirit. Once the capital of British India, it remains a hub of literature, art, music, and theatre, having produced luminaries like Rabindranath Tagore and Satyajit Ray. Nestled along the Hooghly River, the city boasts iconic landmarks such as the Victoria Memorial, Howrah Bridge, and Dakshineswar Kali Temple. With its colonial-era architecture, bustling street life, and famous sweets like rasgulla and mishti doi, Kolkata offers a unique blend of old-world charm and modern vibrancy.

  1. Victoria Memorial – A grand marble museum and monument dedicated to Queen Victoria.
  2. Howrah Bridge – An iconic cantilever bridge connecting Kolkata with Howrah over the Hooghly River.
  3. Dakshineswar Kali Temple – A famous temple dedicated to Goddess Kali, located on the banks of the river.
  4. Indian Museum – The oldest and largest museum in India, housing rare artifacts and exhibits.
  5. Science City – A modern science center with interactive exhibits, a space theater, and educational fun.
  • Kathi Roll – A delicious street snack of paratha wrapped around spicy kebabs, eggs, or paneer.
  • Macher Jhol – A traditional Bengali fish curry made with potatoes, tomatoes, and spices.
  • Rasgulla – Soft, spongy balls made from chhena (cottage cheese) soaked in sugar syrup.
  • Puchka – Kolkata’s version of pani puri, filled with spicy mashed potatoes and tangy tamarind water.
  • Mishti Doi – A sweet, creamy yogurt dessert, typically served chilled and flavored with jaggery.
